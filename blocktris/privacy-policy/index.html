Privacy Policy

Stack Rush / 스택 러시

This privacy policy explains how Stack Rush handles information for the Android app published under package name com.makeitbetter.stackrush.

Effective date: 2026-06-07

1. What the app does

Stack Rush is a single-player arcade game. It is designed to be playable without creating an account or signing in. The app includes optional rewarded ads and an online daily leaderboard.

2. Data stored on your device

The app stores a small amount of gameplay data locally on the device so the game can remember values such as your remaining plays, last recorded height, best recorded height, cumulative total height, and daily leaderboard identity.

This local save data stays on the device unless you remove the app, clear the app's storage through Android settings, or the operating system removes the app data.

3. Online leaderboard

Stack Rush can submit daily leaderboard records to a hosted leaderboard endpoint. The submitted data may include a locally generated player ID, display name, UTC day key, total height, best run height, max combo, score, app version, and submission time.

The display name is generated locally by default and can be changed in the app. It should not contain personal information. The leaderboard is public within the app experience and may show ranked display names and scores to other players.

4. Advertising

Stack Rush uses Google AdMob to provide optional rewarded ads. If you choose to watch a rewarded ad, Google and its advertising partners may process device, ad interaction, diagnostics, and advertising-related data under their own policies. Stack Rush configures ads for a general-audience maximum ad content rating.

5. Data the app does not intentionally collect

Based on the current app implementation, Stack Rush does not intentionally collect or transmit personal information such as your name, email address, phone number, precise location, contacts, photos, microphone input, or in-app account credentials. The app does not include in-game account creation, chat, in-app purchases, or precise location sharing.

6. Permissions, network access, and platform services

The Android app manifest currently declares INTERNET, ACCESS_NETWORK_STATE, and WAKE_LOCK. These are manifest-declared Android permissions included by the current build template and engine setup. They are not presented to the user as Android runtime permission prompts.

Based on the current implementation, the game code does not ask for camera, microphone, contacts, photo library, or precise location access, and it does not implement user accounts, chat, or in-app purchases.

If you download the app through Google Play, Google may process technical or diagnostic information under its own policies when handling downloads, updates, app signing, crash reporting, device compatibility, or store operations. Those platform-level processes are outside the direct control of Stack Rush.

7. Children

Stack Rush is not directed to children and is not designed to knowingly collect personal information from children through in-app forms or account systems. If you believe app data has been handled in a way that conflicts with this policy, contact the publisher so the issue can be reviewed.

8. Changes to this policy

This page may be updated if the app behavior changes, for example if online services, analytics, ads, or account features change in the future. The effective date at the top of this page will also be updated when material changes are published.

9. Data deletion requests

To request deletion of Stack Rush online leaderboard data, email doctorclawler@gmail.com and include the app name, your leaderboard display name, and your locally generated player ID if you can see it in the app. The publisher will review the request and delete matching leaderboard records that can be reasonably identified.

Local gameplay data stored on your device can be deleted by uninstalling the app or clearing the app's storage in Android settings. Advertising data handled by Google AdMob or Google Play is controlled through Google's privacy and advertising controls.

10. Contact

For privacy questions, requests, or notices about Stack Rush, email doctorclawler@gmail.com .
